Cooking at Altitude

When you move to a higher altitude, the boiling point of water drops, due to the lower pressure. This has a profound effect on cooking! Read on to discover the adaptations you'll need to make at high altitude.

Mass vs. Altitude

The relationship between the amount of air there is and the altitude is a question of gravity. Atmospheric particles of nitrogen, hydrogen, and oxygen are held to the Earth because of gravity. The farther from the center of the Earth you move, the fewer atmospheric particles you'll find. Move the altitude slider in the demo to check it out!

Math note: because attraction due to gravity varies inversely with r2, the number of particles decreases at a decreasing rate as you climb in altitude.

Pressure vs. Mass

So now that we know that the amount of air decreases as you move up, the relationship between mass and pressure can be explained. At a constant temperature and volume, a decrease in the amount of air you have creates a decrease in pressure. This is animated to the right.
